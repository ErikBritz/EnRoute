4. Semantic Integration of "Animal Traits"
A recent research gap identified in 2025/2026 is the lack of integration between trait databases (body size, wing shape) and tracking data.

The Project Idea: A "Universal Translator" for migration.

How it works: When a user uploads tracking data, the LBS doesn't just show the path; it calculates a "Cost of Transport" layer. Using remote sensing (slope of terrain, wind resistance), it shows how much energy that specific individual (based on its AVONET data) is spending to move through that specific pixel of data. The "Cool Visuals" (The Render):
The Base Map: Use a 3D terrain map (like Mapbox GL or CesiumJS).

The Trajectories: Instead of flat lines, render trajectories as colored, translucent "ribbons" or "tubes" suspended in the sky (Figure 2, Figure 3).

The Visual Variables:

Color (Hue): Represents the species class (e.g., Raptors are red ribbons, Songbirds are blue).

Color (Opacity/Glow): Represents population density. Where multiple trajectories intersect or cluster, the ribbons glow intensely, forming a "heat mesh" in the air (Figure 1, Figure 8).

Ribbon Width: Represents the velocity of the flow. Fast-moving migrations appear as sleek, narrow ribbons; slower flows are wide and diffuse.

The Dynamic Element: Users can slide a time-bar to see how these 3D structures "shift and weave" from spring to autumn
